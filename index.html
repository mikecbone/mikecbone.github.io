ChessoR

For my dissertation, I was tasked with creating an iPhone application using Swift that involved the use of Artificial Intelligence and search algorithms. I decided to create a Chess application with the ability to play against an AI opponent of varying difficulty. The final app consisted of exactly this along with loading and saving games, support for special moves such as pawn promotion, hot swapping games and saved user statistics. The end focus of the dissertation wasn't just to include a challenging AI to play chess against, but also have the app UX fluid and modern, as expected of the latest apps on the Apple App Store.

JD-MAKS

My university group project also involved mobile development in a team of 6. For this we decided to create two mobile apps, one in Swift for Apple iPhones and one in Java for Android phones. I was tasked with leading the development for the iPhone app as a team of 3. The application involved connecting gym goes and personal trainers, with tracking for personal statistics and records, and a food diary / calorie counter. To support customer user profiles and member to member messaging in the app, a back end of Google's Firebase was used which I was in charge of setting up and maintaining for both the iPhone and Android apps. The Firebase backend also included user sign up, logging in and user authenticification, with handling for errors in all aspects such as email verification on signup, incorrect password on login or unable to authenticate the user due to no service / internet connection.
